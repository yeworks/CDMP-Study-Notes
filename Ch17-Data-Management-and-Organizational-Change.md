## 1. 5 Laws of Change *(p.574)*

| # | Law | Meaning |
|---|-----|---------|
| 1 | **"Organizations don't change, people change"** | 새 조직 발표나 시스템 구현으로 변화 ✗. 사람이 달리 행동할 때 변화 발생. |
| 2 | **"People don't resist change. They resist being changed."** ⭐ | 변화 정의에 참여하거나 비전을 이해하면 더 잘 수용. 독단적 지시는 수용 ✗. |
| 3 | **"Things are the way they are because they got that way"** | 현재 DM 관행의 역사적 이유가 있음. 과거 이해 → 과거 실수 방지. |
| 4 | **"Unless there is push to change, things will likely stay the same"** | Einstein: "You can't solve a problem with the level of thinking that created it." |
| 5 | **"Change would be easy if it weren't for all the people"** | Technology of change = often easy. Challenge = dealing with natural variation in **people**. |

**Change Agents** *(p.574)*: People who pay attention to the **people**, not just the systems. Actively listen to employees, customers, and stakeholders to catch problems before they arise. Requires a clear **VISION** communicated vividly and regularly.

## 2. Bridges's Transition Model — 3 Phases *(p.575)*

> "Most organizations try to start with a beginning, rather than finishing with it. They pay **no attention to endings**. They do not acknowledge the existence of the neutral zone, and then wonder why people have so much difficulty with change." *(William Bridges, 2009)*

⚠️ "The single biggest reason organizational changes fail?" → People driving change **rarely think about endings**.  
⚠️ "Most unpredictable phase?" → **Neutral Zone**

| Phase | Name | Characteristic | Example |
|-------|------|---------------|---------|
| **1** | **The Ending** (Losing / Letting Go) | Acknowledge things we need to let go of. Recognize something is lost. | Changing jobs — loss of close colleagues |
| **2** | **The Neutral Zone** ⭐ | Old way finished but new way not here yet. **Most unpredictable/confusing.** Everything in flux. | Moving to new house — first months |
| **3** | **The New Beginning** | Embedding change. New way feels comfortable, right, the only way. Do NOT force beginning before its time. | Having a baby |

**Transition = Psychological Process** *(p.575)*: All individuals go through all 3 phases, but at **different speeds**. Speed depends on: past experience, personal style, degree of involvement, felt pushed vs moving voluntarily.

**Risk in Neutral Zone**: Slipping back to old habits if not managed.

## 3. Kotter's 8 Errors of Change Management *(p.577–581)*

| Error | Key Content | DM Example |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **#1 Too Much Complacency** | **Biggest mistake.** Plunge ahead without sufficient urgency. Overestimate ability to force change. | "We haven't been fined under current rules." |
| **#2 Failing to Create Guiding Coalition** | Need active support from head of org + coalition of leaders. Without top commitment, self-interest > long-term benefits. | DG requires behavioral changes — leadership essential. |
| **#3 Underestimating Power of Vision** | Urgency + guiding team = useless without clear vision. **Vision ≠ project plan.** Vision = Clear and Compelling Statement. | "We will implement [System X]" ≠ vision. |
| **#4 Under-Communicating Vision** | People won't change unless benefits perceived as significant. Communication in **words AND deeds.** Change message = not much more than **0.5%** of all employee communication. | "Do as I say, not as I do" = kills change effort. |
| **#5 Permitting Obstacles to Block Vision** | 3 types: **Psychological** (fear, knowledge) / **Structural** (job categories, appraisals) / **Active Resistance** (key people refusing) | Performance appraisals forcing people to choose between vision and self-interest. |
| **#6 Failing to Create Short-Term Wins** | Long-term commitment needs regular milestone targets. Must **actively create** short-term wins (not hope for them). | Business Glossary: short-term win = solving specific KPI inconsistency. |
| **#7 Declaring Victory Too Soon** | First performance improvement ≠ success. Until embedded in culture, approaches are **fragile**. Changing a company = **3 to 10 years**. | "We've bought a DQ tool. That's fixed now." |
| **#8 Neglecting Culture Anchoring** | Until embedded in **social norms and shared values**, subject to decay. **"You ignore culture at your peril."** Two keys: (1) show how behaviors influenced performance, (2) embed in next generation of management. | Metadata tagging compliance: initial weeks OK → revert to old habits. |

## 4. Kotter's 8-Stage Process for Major Change *(p.582–597)*

Steps 1–4 = soften entrenched status quo / Steps 5–7 = introduce new practices / Step 8 = lock changes in place.  
**All 8 must be completed — no shortcuts.**

| # | Step | Key Content |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Establishing a Sense of Urgency** | Need **75%** of managers on board. Urgency triggers: regulatory changes, security threats, business continuity risks, mergers. Too much urgency → competing visions or firefighting. |
| 2 | **Creating the Guiding Coalition** | 4 characteristics: **Position Power / Expertise / Credibility / Leadership**. Avoid: **Egos / Snakes** (mistrust) / Reluctant Players |
| 3 | **Developing a Vision and Strategy** | 3 purposes: **Clarification / Motivation / Alignment**. Effective vision = Imaginable / Desirable / Feasible / Focused / Flexible / **Communicable in 5 mins** |
| 4 | **Communicating the Change Vision** | 7 elements: Simple / Metaphor+analogy+example / Multiple forums / **Repeat, repeat, repeat** / Lead by example (walk the talk) / Explain inconsistencies / Two-way communication |
| 5 | **Empowering Broad-Based Action** | Remove roadblocks: Psychological + Structural + Active Resistance. "Smart people" must confront obstacles or others feel disempowered. |
| 6 | **Creating Short-Term Wins** | Actively establish early goals. Regular milestone targets maintain momentum. Short-term wins = collateral for communicating the vision. |
| 7 | **Consolidating Gains and Producing More Change** | Don't declare victory too soon. Keep the process going. Reinforce each step as you move through. |
| 8 | **Anchoring New Approaches in the Culture** | **Most critical for long-term sustainability.** Show how behaviors influenced performance. Embed in **next generation management**. |

**Vision Creation Process** *(p.593)*: First draft by one individual → Guiding Coalition reworks → Teamwork throughout → Both **head AND heart** required → Takes weeks/months/longer → End product: communicable in **5 minutes or less**.

## 5. Gleicher Formula for Change *(p.599)*

> **C = (D × V × F) > R**

| Variable | Stands for | How to Use |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **D** | **Dissatisfaction** with status quo | Increase by exposing problems, showing competitive threats. Use with care — too much increases R. |
| **V** | **Vision** of a concrete and vivid better alternative | Make tangible — what people will start, stop, or do differently. Don't scare people away. |
| **F** | **First Steps** — actionable steps toward vision | Keep achievable. Explicitly link to vision. Iterative prototyping helps. |
| **R** | **Resistance** to change | Act to **reduce resistance**. Avoid alienating people. Understand stakeholders well. |

⚠️ Formula is **multiplicative** (D × V × F). If **any one variable = zero**, the product = zero → change does not overcome resistance.

## 6. Diffusion of Innovations — Rogers (1962) *(p.599–603)*

**Formulated by Everett Rogers in 1962.** Explains how, why, and at what rate **new ideas and technology spread through cultures**.

| Category | % | Characteristic |
|----------|---|---------------|
| **Innovators** | **2.5%** | High risk tolerance. First to spot better ways. Build tentative scorecards using own resources. |
| **Early Adopters** | **13.5%** | **Highest opinion leadership** among all categories. Visionary managers. |
| **Early Majority** | **34%** | Slower adoption. Above average social status. Seldom hold opinion leadership. |
| **Late Majority** | **34%** | Skepticism. Below average social status. Very little opinion leadership. |
| **Laggards** | **16%** | Last to adopt. Averse to change agents. Focus on 'traditions'. May never fully adopt. |

⚠️ "Highest opinion leadership?" → **Early Adopters**

**Tipping Point** *(p.601)*: When innovation is adopted by enough people that it **begins to become mainstream**. Two key challenge points: (1) Breaking past Early Adopter stage. (2) Moving Late Majority into Laggards.

### 5 Adoption Stages *(p.601–602)*

| Stage | Characteristic |
|-------|---------------|
| **Knowledge** | First exposed; lacks info; not inspired to seek more |
| **Persuasion** | Interested; actively seeks info |
| **Decision** | Weighs advantages vs disadvantages; adopt or reject. **Most difficult to obtain empirical evidence.** |
| **Implementation** | Employs; determines usefulness |
| **Confirmation** | Finalizes decision; uses to fullest potential |

### 5 Factors Affecting Acceptance *(p.602–603)*

| Factor | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Relative Advantage** | Does the innovation offer advantage over previous way? (key question) |
| **Compatibility** | Compatible with their life/way of working? |
| **Complexity** | If too difficult, less likely to be adopted |
| **Trialability** | How easy to experiment (freemium, prototypes) |
| **Observability** | Visibility drives communication through networks |

**4 Key Elements of Diffusion** *(p.601)*: Innovation / Communication Channels / Time / Social System

## 7. Communicating Data Management Value *(p.605–608)*

### Communication Plan Elements *(Table 39, p.608)*

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| **Message** | Information to be conveyed |
| **Goal / Objective** | Desired outcome |
| **Audience** | Group or individual targeted |
| **Style** | Formality + detail level tailored to audience |
| **Channel / Method** | Web, email, meetings, workshops |
| **Timing** | When and in relation to what events |
| **Frequency** | Repetition schedule |
| **Communicators** | Who delivers — person has profound influence |
| **Expected Response** | Anticipate reactions; formulate responses in advance |
| **Metrics** | Surveys, interviews, focus groups. **Changes in behavior = ultimate test** |

**Audience Tactics** *(p.606–607)*: Solve problems / Address pain points / Present changes as improvements / Have a vision of success / Avoid jargon / Share stories / Recognize fear as motivation.

**Group Think Warning** *(p.589)*: Psychological effect in highly coherent groups — everyone agrees even with reservations. Prevention: Scientific method / criteria for decisions / **leaders speak last** / seek outside knowledge.
